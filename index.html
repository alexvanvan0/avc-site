The Problem

New role. New club.
None of your records follow.

3

coaches. One club.
Zero shared records.

The average volleyball club changes head coach every 2–3 seasons. Each transition wipes the institutional memory — injury history, readiness patterns, supplement logs — because the sport has no standard way to store it somewhere that survives.

1
The data lives in people
Injury history, readiness patterns, medication notes — in a coach's head or a personal spreadsheet with no standard way to hand it over.
2
The handoff never happens
No standard format, no export. The new coach starts from conversation and observation alone.
3
Players pay the price
Lost injury history. Missed supplement flags. Fatigue misread as attitude. Players re-prove everything to someone new.

Core Features

Everything.
In one place.

F-01
Player & Team CRM
Structured, searchable player records — stats history, training goals, coach notes, positional profile. The knowledge that stays with the club. Everything a new coach needs, on day one.
F-02
Health, Medical & Anti-Doping
Injury history, recovery timelines, return-to-play status. Supplement and medication logs with anti-doping compliance flags. Role-gated — only medical staff see sensitive records.

Performance Dashboard
Team and player trend visualisation — kill %, block success, reception accuracy, errors per set. Filterable by match, player, date. Correlated with self-reported wellness data.

Interactive court diagram for rotations and formations. Session builder with drill library. Plan, log, and track training load — all connected to player records.

For Players

60 seconds.
Before every session.

The player portal runs on any phone — no app install. Four sliders, one question, done. Then your personal dashboard: stats, coach note, medical status, drill suggestions.

The check-in is four sliders and a button. Under 60 seconds, every time. The simplicity is intentional — if it feels like homework, players stop doing it and the whole system falls apart.

After submitting, players land on a personal home screen — not an empty confirmation page. Their own stats, a note the coach has chosen to share, medical status from the physio, and two drill suggestions based on their development areas.

Works on any phone browser. No download. No account creation beyond the first login. Walk through the full flow using the prototype →

Open Source

No lock-in.
Ever.

AVC is open source by design, not as an afterthought. Your data belongs to your club — always exportable, always readable, no dependency on AVC's infrastructure.

01
Full data ownership
Export everything — JSON or PDF — at any time. Your records belong to your club, not to AVC.
02
No pricing surprises
The core platform is free and open source. No per-seat fees, no feature gates on the essentials.
03
Self-hostable
Clubs or federations can host their own instance. No dependence on AVC's infrastructure required.
04
Community-driven
All code on GitHub. Contributions welcome. The platform improves with every club that uses it.

Why does open source matter for a coaching tool?Most sports software locks your data in proprietary formats and charges more every year to access it. When they shut down — and some do — you lose everything.

AVC is built differently. The full codebase is published under an open licence. Data exports are human-readable JSON and PDF. If AVC ever disappears, your records survive independently.

For clubs without large budgets, open source also means zero cost to adopt. No procurement process, no annual contract negotiation. A link and a setup call.

FAQ

Questions clubs
always ask.

Do players need to download an app?+
No. The player portal is a web page that works on any phone browser — Safari, Chrome, anything. Players bookmark the link or you pin it in your team WhatsApp. No app store, no installation, no update prompts.
Is this GDPR compliant?+
AVC is designed with GDPR principles from the ground up. Health and medical data is role-gated and encrypted at rest. Players consent to data logging when they register. Full data export and deletion is available at any time.
What happens to our data if AVC shuts down?+
Because AVC is open source and built on standard technologies (PostgreSQL, React, Python), your data never becomes inaccessible. Export everything as JSON or PDF at any time. Clubs with technical staff can also self-host the entire platform — no dependency on AVC's servers.
How long does setup take?+
The platform is live within 48 hours of the requirements call. Onboarding staff and creating player profiles takes one session — typically two hours on-site. Most clubs have their full squad checking in daily within the first week.
Can coaches see all sections — youth, men's, women's?+
Yes. AVC supports multi-section clubs with role-based access. The head coach of each section sees their squad. A club director sees all sections. Medical staff see health records across all sections. Access is configured per person during onboarding.
Will AVC give medical advice to players?+
No. AVC is a coaching and management tool, not a medical platform. Status updates visible to players — like "cleared to train" or "see physio before Thursday" — are written by your medical staff, not generated by the platform. AVC never produces automated medical recommendations.
Is there a cost after the pilot?+
The pilot is completely free. After the pilot period, hosting costs approximately €75/month for a full club. Pilot clubs get preferential terms. The core platform remains open source and self-hostable at no cost.
What if players don't bother checking in?+
The check-in takes under 60 seconds — four sliders and one question. Coaches see a "who hasn't checked in today" view and can follow up before training. On-site onboarding in the first two weeks is specifically focused on making it habitual, not just taught once and forgotten.
